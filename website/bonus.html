9. What’s the difference between a black box and white box test?

The main difference between black box and white box test is the availability of information provided to the penetration tester before the test has begun.

For example, in a white box test the tester may get told detailed information about the client’s servers, such as what services they are running, all the IP addresses being used etc.

Black box on the other hand is where the penetration tester is kept in the dark and not told any information at all.

They may not even be given an IP address, they may only be told to just investigate company xyz. Because of this perspective, the penetration tester is like a real attacker in a black box.
Information like out-of-scope targets will still be provided to the tester for legal reasons.

10. If I wanted to look at a deleted page, what website would I use?

You would use a website (like) the waybackmachine. Using the waybackmachine you can find deleted pages.
Under certain circumstances, you may even find sensitive deleted files/pages (that someone accidentally uploaded then proceeded to delete).

11. Name a popular vulnerability database

The two most popular vulnerability databases are ExploitDB and NVD. For more on ExploitDB check out the ExploitDB page.

NVD (national vulnerability database) is owned by the US government and is basically a big library full of CVEs and its CVSS scoring (severity scoring).

12. In this scenario, you are part of an organisation hired to do a penetration test. When investigating a client’s server, you detect a vulnerability, however it is out of scope. What do you do?

You should contact the client and bring it up with them. They will either say leave it (and possibly have it investigated in a future test) or exploit it.
If they say exploit it, the scoping contract (made before the penetration testing starts) will need to be amended to take this activity into consideration (or at the very least be put on the record so the client is well aware they told you to investigate it).

13. Following on from task 12, as you are investigating a client’s server you’ve notice the client has been undergoing illegal activity (for example, found evidence of illegal images on the servers). What do you do?

As the client is not above the law, the authorities will need to be contacted. If you do not act upon it and it was later found you kept silent about it you may also suffer legal consequences, and as such it would be best to get into contact with someone regarding your findings.
